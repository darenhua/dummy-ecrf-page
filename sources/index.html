Instructions

Weight scales will be provided to the study sites and the same scale should be used to the
extent possible for all measurements. Using the same scale will help to minimise the
variability in body weight measurements. The equipment must be calibrated according to
manufacturer's specifications. Weight should be recorded in kilograms up to 1 decimal.
The body weight must be measured in fasting state (except the screening visit). If the trial
participant is not fasted, the participant should be called in for a new visit within the visit
window. In order to get comparable body weight values, shoes, coats/jackets, and any
headgear should be taken off, and pockets should be emptied of heavy objects (i.e. keys,
coins etc.). Headgear worn for religious reasons are acceptable, but this should be
consistently worn for all weight measurements in the trial. The trial participant should empty
the bladder before weight is measured.

Checking and Managing GI Symptoms

  • +
 
0

Instructions

For trial participants who experience intolerable GI symptoms, for example nausea, vomiting,
or diarrhea, the following steps shall be taken:
- First, the trial participant should be counseled on dietary behaviors that may help
mitigate nausea and vomiting. This may include eating smaller meals, splitting
3 daily meals into 4 or more smaller ones, and stop eating when they feel full. When
a trial participant experiences vomiting or diarrhea, the participant should be
instructed to stay well hydrated.
- If symptoms persist, the trial participant should be prescribed symptomatic
medication (antiemetic or antidiarrheal medication) at the discretion of the
investigator and be kept hydrated.
- A temporary interruption of the IMP for 1 dose is permitted.
- A temporary interruption of a second dose of the IMP may be allowed at the
investigator's discretion.
- The IMP should be resumed at the assigned dose immediately, either alone or in
combination with symptomatic medication, which can also be utilized to manage
symptoms.
- If, between Weeks 16 and 32 of treatment, the symptoms persist in spite of these
measures, down-titration and subsequent re-escalation is allowed (see Table 5). For
participants randomised to 6.0 mg who are not able to attain or stay at the
maintenance dose of 6.0 mg, a dose reduction to 3.6 mg (or to 2.4 mg in exceptional
circumstances) is possible after consultation with a sponsor representative. For
participants randomised to 3.6 mg who are not able to attain or stay at the
maintenance dose of 3.6 mg, a dose reduction to 2.4 mg would be possible in
exceptional circumstances after consultation with a sponsor representative. This
should only be allowed if the trial participant would otherwise discontinue the IMP
and if considered safe to continue the IMP, as per the investigator's discretion.
Thorough documentation of all measures taken to improve tolerability, including
counseling on dietary behaviors, symptomatic medication (antiemetic or
antidiarrheal), temporary interruption of IMP, and outcomes of the discussion with
sponsor representative, is required in a specific eCRF page in case of a dose
reduction. For further details, please refer to Appendix 10.1. IMP might be dispensed
during an unscheduled visit.
- If the symptoms become tolerable or are completely resolved, and as per
investigator's discretion, it is recommended that the trial participant makes up to 2 attempts to re-escalate per dose escalation scheme to the assigned dose level. In
such cases, the entire dose escalation period should not be longer than 32 weeks.
- If a trial participant does not tolerate the higher dose level after re-escalation or in
case re-escalation is not considered safe as per investigator's discretion, the trial
participant may stay at a lower dose level of 3.6 mg (or 2.4 mg in exceptional
circumstances) once weekly until EOT (Table 5).
- If the last dose was 2.4 mg or less, or if GI symptoms intractable by the measures
described above persist, then the IMP has to be discontinued (Table 5).
The management process for intolerable GI symptoms is graphically displayed in
Appendix 10.1.
